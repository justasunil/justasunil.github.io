About me

I am a Ph.D. scholar at HiPeC Lab, Indraprastha Institute of Information Technology Delhi (IIIT Delhi), under the guidance of Dr. Vivek Kumar. Prior to this, I worked at the HiPeC Lab as a Research Assistant, where I focused on designing and implementing energy-efficient runtimes for distributed systems using hybrid programming models.

Before starting my Ph.D., I completed my B.Tech in Computer Science and Engineering at IIIT Delhi. As an undergraduate researcher at the HiPeC Lab, I worked on a project aimed at designing an energy-efficient runtime library for parallel programs running on multi-core processors.

My research interests lie in High Performance Computing (HPC), with a focus on building runtime software for HPC systems composed of multicore processors. Specifically, I am interested in developing adaptive runtime systems that can dynamically adjust to power-efficient environments on HPC systems without compromising performance.
