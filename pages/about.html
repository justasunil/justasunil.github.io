About me

I am a Ph.D. scholar in HiPeC Lab at Indraprastha Institute of Information Technology, Delhi (IIIT-Delhi), guided by Dr. Vivek Kumar (Assistant Professor at IIIT-Delhi). I also worked as a Research Assistant in HiPec Lab where I was looking for designing and implementing an energy-efficient runtime for distributed systems using Hybrid programming models.

Before that, I was a B.Tech student in the computer science and engineering department at IIIT-Delhi. I worked on a thesis as an undergraduate researcher in the final year of my graduation. My thesis aimed to design an energy-efficient runtime library for parallel programs on multi-core processors.

My Research Interest lies in High Performance Computing and building Parallel programming models and efficient Runtime systems. Particularly, I am interested to design an adaptive runtime model that dynamically adapts power-controlled environments on HPC systems without losing significant performance.
