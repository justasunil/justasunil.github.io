About me

I am a Ph.D. scholar, working with HiPeC Lab at Indraprastha Institute of Information Technology, Delhi (IIIT-Delhi), guided by Dr. Vivek Kumar (Assistant Professor at IIIT-Delhi). I also worked at HiPeC Lab as a Research Assistant where I was looking for designing and implementing an energy-efficient runtime for distributed systems using Hybrid programming models.

Before that, I was a B.Tech student in the computer science and engineering department at IIIT-Delhi. I worked on a research project in HiPeC Lab as an undergraduate researcher. My role in project was to design an energy-efficient runtime library for parallel programs on multi-core processors.

My Research Interest lies in High Performance Computing and building runtime softwares for HPC systems composed with multicore processors. Particularly, I am interested to design an adaptive runtime software that dynamically adapts power-efficient environments on HPC systems without hurting performance.
