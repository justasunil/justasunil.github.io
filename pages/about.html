About me

I am a Ph.D. scholar in the Department of Computer Science at Indraprastha Institute of Information Technology, Delhi (IIIT-Delhi), guided by Dr. Vivek Kumar (Assistant Professor at IIIT-Delhi) and co-guided by Dr. Sridutt Bhalachandra (Postdoctoral Appointee at Argonne National Laboratory). Also, I am a Research Assistant in HiPeC Lab at IIIT-Delhi. My current aim is to design and implement energy-efficient runtime for distributed systems using Hybrid programming models.

My Research Interest lies in High Performance Computing and building Parallel programming models and efficient Runtime systems. I aim to design an adaptive runtime model that dynamically adapts power-controlled environments on many-core systems without losing significant performance.

Before that, I was a B.Tech student in the computer science and engineering department at IIIT-Delhi. I worked on a thesis as an undergraduate researcher in the final of my graduation. My thesis aimed to design an energy-efficient runtime library for parallel programs on multi-core processors.
